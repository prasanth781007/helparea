Who We Are

About Adi Parampara

✦

Adi Parampara is a grassroots non-profit weaving together culture, dignity, and opportunity for tribal communities across India. We don't arrive with solutions — we listen first.

Our name — Adi Parampara — means "the original tradition." We believe ancient knowledge, oral histories, and indigenous art are irreplaceable gifts humanity cannot afford to lose.

Founded in 2010, we work in 50+ villages across 6 states, building programs designed by communities, for communities.

What We Do

Our Core Programs

✦
📚
Program 01

Education & Literacy

Bringing quality education to remote tribal schools — building classrooms, training teachers, and supporting children through scholarships and mid-day meals.

Learn more →
🏥
Program 02

Healthcare Outreach

Mobile health camps, maternal care, vaccination drives, and telemedicine connecting tribal villages to doctors — because geography should never determine health outcomes.

Learn more →
🌸
Program 03

Women Empowerment

Self-help groups, skill training, microfinance access, and legal awareness programs helping tribal women build financial independence and reclaim their rights.

Learn more →
🎭
Program 04

Cultural Preservation

Documenting folk songs, dances, and oral histories. Running festivals and arts workshops that keep ancestral traditions alive and economically valued.

Learn more →
🧶
Program 05

Livelihood & Artisans

Market linkages, e-commerce training, and fair-trade certification for tribal artisans — ensuring traditional crafts generate dignified income in the modern economy.

Learn more →
🌾
Program 06

Food & Nutrition

Community gardens, nutrition awareness, and food distribution ensuring no child in our villages experiences hunger or malnutrition.

Our Story

The Journey So Far

✦
2010

Founded in Jharkhand

Adi Parampara begins with 5 team members in 3 villages — documenting oral histories and establishing a community library.

✦
✦
2013

First School Built

With donor support we built the first school in the Saranda forest region — 120 children enrolled in the first term.

2016

Healthcare Program Launched

Mobile health vans begin monthly visits to 20 remote villages. Over 3,000 patients received care in year one.

✦
✦
2019

FCRA Registration & Expansion

Awarded FCRA status enabling international donations. Expanded to Odisha, Madhya Pradesh, and Chhattisgarh.

2022

Artisan E-Commerce Platform

Launched an online marketplace connecting 300+ artisans to buyers — ₹40L+ in first-year sales.

✦
✦
2024

Today & Beyond

50+ villages, 6 states, 5,000+ donors. Next goal: reaching 100 villages by 2027.
